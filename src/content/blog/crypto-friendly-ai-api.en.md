# Using AI APIs Without a Credit Card: How Crypto-Friendly Providers Work

You do not need a credit card to use AI APIs. Crypto-friendly providers accept USDT payments directly, letting developers start with as little as $2—no card, no subscription, no geographic restrictions.

If you have ever been blocked from signing up for an AI API because your card was declined, your region was unsupported, or you simply prefer not to share banking details, crypto payments can remove that barrier entirely. Here is how it works in practice.

---

## Why Credit Cards Are a Bottleneck for Some Developers

Most AI API providers require a credit card on file. For many developers, this creates real friction:

- **Card availability.** Not everyone has a credit card. In many regions, debit cards are the norm, and international credit cards can be difficult to obtain.
- **Declined transactions.** Cross-border payments frequently trigger fraud detection. Legitimate purchases get blocked, requiring support tickets and waiting.
- **Geographic restrictions.** Some providers restrict service to specific countries, regardless of payment method.
- **Privacy preferences.** Some developers prefer not to link their identity to every service they try—especially during prototyping.

These are not edge cases. They are routine obstacles for developers outside North America and Western Europe, and for anyone experimenting with multiple API providers.

---

## How Crypto Payments Solve This

Crypto-friendly AI API providers replace the card requirement with USDT deposits. The workflow is straightforward:

1. **Sign up** for an account (typically just an email).
2. **Choose a top-up amount** from preset tiers—on apilane, the options are $2, $5, $10, and $20.
3. **Send USDT** from your personal wallet to the provided deposit address.
4. **Start making API calls** once the on-chain transaction confirms.

No credit check. No billing address. No monthly invoice. You prepay for what you need and use it at your own pace.

The reason USDT (Tether) is commonly used rather than other cryptocurrencies is stability. USDT is designed to maintain a 1:1 peg with the US dollar, so your $2 deposit is worth approximately $2 of API credit—no conversion guessing required.

## TRC20 vs BSC: Which Network to Use

apilane supports two USDT networks: **TRC20 (Tron)** and **BSC (BNB Smart Chain)**. Both work, but there are practical differences.

| | TRC20 (Tron) | BSC (BNB Smart Chain) |
|--|-------------|----------------------|
| Transaction fees | Very low (typically under $1) | Low (typically $0.10–$0.50) |
| Confirmation speed | ~3 minutes | ~5 seconds (block time) |
| Wallet support | Widely supported (Trust Wallet, TronLink, etc.) | Widely supported (MetaMask, Trust Wallet, etc.) |
| Best for | Small deposits where fee ratio matters | Users who already hold BNB or use BSC-based DeFi |

For a $2 top-up, TRC20 is often the practical choice because its fees are negligible relative to the deposit amount. For larger top-ups, either network works fine.

**One critical note:** always send on the network you selected. Sending TRC20 tokens to a BSC address (or vice versa) typically results in permanent loss of funds. The deposit page will display the correct network—verify it before confirming.

## No Refunds, No Problem: Why Small Top-Ups Matter

Here is something to be upfront about: crypto deposits are generally not refundable. Once you send USDT to a deposit address, the transaction cannot be reversed.

This is not a policy quirk—it is a fundamental property of blockchain transactions. No provider can "undo" an on-chain transfer.

The practical solution is simple: **deposit only what you plan to use soon.** On apilane, the minimum top-up is $2, which is enough for over 1,300 calls on the most affordable model. There is no pressure to deposit more than you need.

Some tips for managing your balance:

- **Start small.** Use the $2 tier to test the API and confirm it fits your workflow.
- **Top up as you go.** When your balance runs low, deposit another small amount.
- **Monitor your usage.** The dashboard shows remaining balance and per-call costs.
- **Do not treat it as a wallet.** Keep your USDT in your own custody until you need to spend it.

## What to Look for in a Crypto-Friendly AI API Provider

If you are evaluating providers that accept crypto, here are practical things to check:

- **Minimum deposit.** A low minimum ($2–$5) means you can test without committing significant funds. High minimums lock you in before you know if the service works for you.
- **Payment networks.** TRC20 and BSC are the most common for USDT. More network options mean more flexibility.
- **No subscription required.** Prepaid, pay-as-you-go is the standard for crypto-friendly providers. If a provider requires a monthly subscription paid in crypto, that is a different model with its own trade-offs.
- **Pricing transparency.** You should be able to see per-call or per-token rates before depositing. If prices are hidden behind a paywall, that is a red flag.
- **Dashboard and monitoring.** A functional dashboard where you can track balance, usage, and per-call costs is essential—especially when you are managing a prepaid balance.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I deposit USDT to my account?

After signing in, go to the dashboard and select a top-up amount ($2, $5, $10, or $20). You will receive a wallet address. Send USDT from your own wallet to that address. Once the transaction is confirmed on-chain, your balance updates automatically.

### TRC20 or BSC—which network should I use?

Both are supported. TRC20 (Tron) tends to have lower transaction fees, which matters more for small top-ups. BSC (BNB Smart Chain) can be convenient if you already hold assets there. The key is to send on the correct network—sending on the wrong network may result in lost funds.

### What happens if I send funds on the wrong network?

Funds sent on an unsupported network or to an incorrect address typically cannot be recovered. Always double-check the network before confirming a transaction.

### Can I get a refund on my USDT deposit?

No. Crypto transactions are irreversible by nature, so deposits cannot be refunded. That is why apilane offers low minimum top-ups starting at $2—deposit only what you need, when you need it.

### Is BTC or any other cryptocurrency supported?

Currently, apilane only accepts USDT via TRC20 and BSC networks.
